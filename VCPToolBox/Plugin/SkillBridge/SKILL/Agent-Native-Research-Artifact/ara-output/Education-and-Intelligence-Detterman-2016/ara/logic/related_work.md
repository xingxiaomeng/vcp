# Related Work

## Foundational / Pre-20th Century
- **Huarte (1698)** — *Examen de ingenios*: Early recognition that different intellectual capacities suit different fields of study. *Role in paper*: Historical anchor — the importance of student characteristics was understood centuries ago.
- **Galton (1869)** — *Hereditary Genius*: Pioneered the study of individual differences in cognitive ability. *Role in paper*: Historical root of the intelligence research tradition.
- **Binet & Simon (1905)** — Early intelligence testing. *Role in paper*: Origins of the modern intelligence measurement tradition.
- **Spearman (1904)** — "General intelligence": Proposed the g factor. *Role in paper*: Conceptual foundation for general cognitive ability.

## The Coleman Report and Its Legacy
- **Coleman et al. (1966)** — *Equality of Educational Opportunity*. *Role in paper*: Primary empirical cornerstone — first large-scale demonstration that schools account for little variance. [EXTENDS] the problem.
- **Jencks et al. (1972)** — *Inequality: A Reassessment*. *Role in paper*: Confirmed and extended Coleman's findings. [SUPPORTS] C01, C02.
- **Gamoran & Long (2006)** — 40-year retrospective. *Role in paper*: Extended the temporal scope. [SUPPORTS] C02.
- **Heyneman & Loxley (1983)** — Heyneman-Loxley effect. *Role in paper*: Identified an important qualification — schools matter more in low-income countries. [BOUNDS] C02.
- **Baker, Goesling, & LeTendre (2002)** — Diminishing Heyneman-Loxley effect. *Role in paper*: Shows the effect is shrinking. [SUPPORTS] C05.

## Teacher Effects
- **Chingos & Whitehurst (2014)** — School superintendents. *Role in paper*: Most direct and recent estimate of teacher variance (3.0% out of 9.2% school variance). [SUPPORTS] C01.
- **Whitehurst, Chingos, & Gallaher (2013)** — School district effects. *Role in paper*: Alternative estimate (teachers 6.7% out of 9.6% total school variance). [SUPPORTS] C01.
- **Byrne et al. (2010)** — Twin study of teacher effects. *Role in paper*: Upper bound estimate using twin design (≤ 8%). [SUPPORTS] C01.
- **The New Teacher Project (2015)** — Teacher training costs and ineffectiveness. *Role in paper*: Contextual support — teacher training is expensive ($18K/yr) and shows limited impact.
- **Jacob, Lefgren, & Sims (2010)** — Low persistence of teacher effects. *Role in paper*: 75%+ of teacher-induced gains fade within one year. [SUPPORTS] C01.
- **Coalition for Evidence-Based Policy (2013)** — Only 7 of 77 RCTs showed positive effects. *Role in paper*: Supports limited effectiveness of educational interventions targeting school/teacher factors.

## College Effects
- **Angoff & Johnson (1990)** — SAT → GRE. *Role in paper*: Shows student characteristics predict 93% of GRE variance; institution ≤ 7%. [SUPPORTS] C01, C02.
- **Dale & Krueger (2011)** — College selectivity and earnings. *Role in paper*: Attending more selective colleges doesn't increase earnings after correcting for student ability (except for minorities/first-generation). [SUPPORTS] C02.
- **Bowen & Bok (1998)** — *The Shape of the River*. *Role in paper*: Supports social networking explanation for minority/first-generation benefits.

## Intelligence and Achievement
- **Deary et al. (2007)** — English adolescents (GCSE & CAT). *Role in paper*: Primary evidence for intelligence → achievement link. [SUPPORTS] C03.
- **Kaufman et al. (2012)** — Cross-battery replication. *Role in paper*: Replicates Deary at al. with different tests. [SUPPORTS] C03.
- **Lynn & Mikk (2007)** — National-level TIMSS. *Role in paper*: Ecological-level confirmation. [SUPPORTS] C03.
- **Krapohl et al. (2014)** — Twin study of 9 student characteristics. *Role in paper*: Comprehensive comparison showing intelligence dominates other student traits. [SUPPORTS] C03, C06.

## Causal Direction
- **Crano, Kenny, & Campbell (1972)** — Cross-lagged panel. *Role in paper*: Foundational causal evidence. [SUPPORTS] C04.
- **Watkins, Lei, & Canivez (2007)** — WISC-III cross-lagged replication. *Role in paper*: Replication with latent variables. [SUPPORTS] C04.
- **Mosing et al. (2014)** — Swedish twin music study. *Role in paper*: Analogous evidence — practice doesn't cause ability. [SUPPORTS] C04.
- **Ceci (1991)** — Schooling and intelligence. *Role in paper*: Acknowledges negative environments can reduce intelligence, qualifying the strong causal claim. [BOUNDS] C04.

## Flynn Effect
- **Flynn (1984)** — IQ gains over generations. *Role in paper*: Evidence that environmental factors can boost test scores. [BOUNDS] C04.
- **Tuddenham (1948)** — Soldier intelligence in WWI and WWII. *Role in paper*: Early evidence of IQ gains. [BOUNDS] C04.

## Genetics of Intelligence
- **Thompson, Detterman, & Plomin (1991)** — Western Reserve Twin Study. *Role in paper*: Early evidence of genetic overlap between intelligence and achievement. [SUPPORTS] C03.
- **Petrill & Wilkerson (2000)** — Behavioral genetics review. *Role in paper*: Environment more important for younger children; genes become increasingly important with age. [SUPPORTS] C03.
- **Luo, Thompson, & Detterman (2003)** — Mental speed as causal factor. *Role in paper*: Identifies a mechanism linking g to achievement (processing speed). [SUPPORTS] C03.
- **Haworth et al. (2010)** — Heritability increases with age. *Role in paper*: Key genetic finding — heritability of g increases linearly from childhood to adulthood. [SUPPORTS] C03.
- **Plomin, DeFries, Knopik, & Neiderhiser (2016)** — Top 10 replicated behavioral genetics findings. *Role in paper*: Summary of consensus findings. [SUPPORTS] C03, C06.

## Neuroscience of Intelligence
- **Jung & Haier (2007)** — P-FIT model. *Role in paper*: Neurobiological model of intelligence. [SUPPORTS] C06.
- **Basten, Hilger, & Fiebach (2015)** — P-FIT extension. *Role in paper*: Updated meta-analytic confirmation. [SUPPORTS] C06.
- **Colom, Jung, & Haier (2006)** — g in brain structure. *Role in paper*: Specific brain regions associated with g. [SUPPORTS] C06.
- **Giedd et al. (1999)** — Brain development trajectories. *Role in paper*: Developmental context for intelligence. [SUPPORTS] C06.

## Cognitive Structure
- **Johnson & Bouchard (2005a, 2005b)** — VPR model. *Role in paper*: Statistically validated structural model of intelligence. [SUPPORTS] C06.
- **Johnson et al. (2004, 2008)** — g is consistent across batteries. *Role in paper*: Stability of g factor across test batteries. [SUPPORTS] C03.
- **Kyllonen & Christal (1990)** — Working memory and reasoning. *Role in paper*: Working memory as core of reasoning ability. [SUPPORTS] C06.
- **Engelhardt et al. (2015)** — Executive function heritability. *Role in paper*: Genetic basis of executive functions. [SUPPORTS] C06.
- **Deary et al. (2000)** — Stability from childhood to old age. *Role in paper*: Demonstrates lifelong stability of intelligence. [SUPPORTS] C03.

## Educational Practice
- **Asbury & Plomin (2013)** — *G is for Genes*. *Role in paper*: Applied behavioral genetics for educators. [SUPPORTS] C06.
- **Detterman (2014)** — Expertise and ability. *Role in paper*: Author's own critique of deliberate practice theory. [SUPPORTS] C03.
- **Johnson (2016)** — "The teacher is the most significant variable." *Role in paper*: Representative quote of teacher-centric view that the paper opposes. [REFUTED BY] C01, C02.
